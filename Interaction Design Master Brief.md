# Project Brief: Interfaces That Feel
**NCAD MA Interaction Design Studio Brief**

## Working Title
**Interfaces That Feel: Designing for Emotion in Digital Spaces**

## Background
For decades, digital interaction design has largely optimized for function: usability, efficiency, clarity, and consistency. Especially in Western product ecosystems, minimalism and familiar interaction patterns have become dominant. While this has created scalable and accessible systems, it has also contributed to a landscape of increasingly uniform interfaces.

This brief asks a different question:

**What happens when we design not only for function, but for feeling?**

Many of the digital spaces we inhabit every day are deeply personal: messaging apps, music platforms, journaling spaces, dating apps, social media, and creative tools. These are places where people process emotion, build relationships, express identity, and seek connection. Yet the interfaces themselves often remain emotionally neutral.

This project invites students to explore how digital interactions might create atmosphere, emotional resonance, ambiguity, intimacy, joy, discomfort, curiosity, or reflection.

The challenge is not to abandon usability, but to critically examine when and where emotional experience should become a design material in itself.

---

## Design Challenge
**How can designed experiences make people feel something real?**

Rather than beginning with tasks, flows, and conversion goals, begin with a **feeling state**.

Examples may include, but are not limited to:
- connection between strangers
- comfort during loneliness
- collective joy
- anticipation
- nostalgia
- playful intimacy
- grief or healing
- wonder and curiosity
- emotional reflection
- ritual and belonging

Your project should explore how interaction design translates emotional qualities into behaviors, rhythms, material qualities, and patterns of use — regardless of medium.

Students should investigate how emotion can be embedded through:
- interaction flows and pacing
- motion, transition, and feedback
- sound, haptics, or physical form
- visual or spatial language
- ambiguity vs clarity
- social presence and shared experience
- ritualized or repeated interactions

The outcome may be a digital product, a physical object, a hybrid experience, a speculative concept, or a poetic experiment. Medium follows intention.

---

## Opportunity Areas
Students may choose one of the following routes:

### 1. Reimagine an Existing Experience
Take an existing touchpoint — digital or physical — and redesign it around an emotional goal rather than a functional one.

Example prompts:
- What would a music platform feel like if it was designed around collective joy?
- How might a waiting room be redesigned around calm instead of anxiety?
- What would a dating app feel like if it prioritized softness over performance?

### 2. Design for a Specific Emotional Moment
Create a concept that holds a single, precise emotional experience at its centre.

Examples:
- the feeling of standing in a crowd at a concert
- the intimacy of a shared glance
- the calm after emotional chaos

### 3. Interaction Poem / Experimental Artefact
Students may create a smaller, poetic work that behaves more like an experiment than a product.

This route explicitly welcomes unconventional, weird, and artistically-driven ideas across any medium.

---

## Expected Outputs
- research framing and emotional territory
- concept statement and interaction principles
- documentation of the design process and key decisions
- a prototype, artefact, or experiential outcome at appropriate fidelity
- rationale explaining the emotional intention
- reflection on the relationship between feeling and function

Form and fidelity are open. Outcomes may include Figma prototypes, coded experiments, physical objects, motion studies, interactive installations, or hybrid artefacts.

---

## Critical Reflection
Students should critically reflect on:
- when emotional ambiguity enriches an experience
- when clarity must remain dominant
- ethical implications of designing for emotional influence
- differences between designing for feeling and manipulating behavior

---

## Learning Outcomes
By the end of this project, students should be able to:
- use emotion as a design material
- articulate experiential design intent
- prototype atmosphere and affect through interaction
- critically challenge dominant usability conventions
- explore interaction design as both product and expressive medium

---

## Provocation
**Life is messy. Why do our interfaces pretend otherwise?**
